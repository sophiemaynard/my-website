The Artist

From my studio in Berkhamsted, I create works that serve as the emotional and aesthetic anchor of a room. My work is shaped by a life spent outdoors and the quiet inspiration I find in my whippet, Trudy, and my horse, Geenie. A portrait should capture more than a physical likeness; it should reflect the character of a companion. I specialise in pet portraiture that honors the unique bond you share with your animals. My goal is to create a meaningful tribute that feels as alive as the subject itself.

I also create site-specific artwork designed to suit the atmosphere of a room. Whether it is a personal residence or a professional environment, I believe art should be the focal point that brings a space together. I work with you to ensure the scale and style of the piece are perfectly balanced with its surroundings.

To ensure each project receives the focus it requires, I accept only a select number of commissions each year. This allows me to maintain a high standard of craftsmanship and work closely with every patron from the first sketch to the final piece.

The Philosophy

I view every commission as a site-specific dialogue. A work of art must resonate with the architecture and atmosphere it inhabits. By considering light, scale, and the intended energy of a room, from the sanctuary of a home to the identity of a professional space, I ensure that each piece becomes a seamless, intentional extension of the interior.
